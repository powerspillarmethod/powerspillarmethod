Welcome & Set the Tone

⏱ 0–5 minutes

"The first 5 minutes set the emotional tone for the entire 12 weeks. Your client is nervous. Your job is to make them feel safe, seen, and excited — in that order."

Opening Script
Say this
"Hey [Name], it's so great to finally meet you. I'm Wes — I'm really glad you're here. Before we dive in, I just want you to know that this is a completely judgment-free space. There's nothing you can say that will surprise me, and nothing is off limits. The only rule is honesty — even if it's uncomfortable. That's where the real work happens."
Ask this
"How are you feeling right now — about today, about starting this? Be honest."
If they say...
"Nervous / scared"
"That's exactly right. Nervous means it matters to you. Let's use that energy."
"Excited / ready"
"I love that. Let's make sure we channel that energy into something that actually lasts this time."
"Skeptical / not sure"
"Honestly? Good. Skepticism means you've tried things before and been let down. We're going to do this differently."
Do this
Take 30 seconds of silence after they answer. Let them feel heard. Resist the urge to fill the space immediately. Nod, make eye contact, then say: "Thank you for sharing that. I hear you."
Motivation Check
Ask this
"Tell me — what's the real reason you're here? Not the surface answer. The 3am reason. The thing that made you finally say 'enough.'"
Coach notes
Listen for
The emotional driver beneath the goal. "I want to lose weight" → dig deeper → "I want to feel confident in my body again." That's their real why. Write it down — you'll come back to it in Week 12.

The 4-Pillar Philosophy

⏱ 5–12 minutes

"This is your framework introduction. Keep it conversational — not a lecture. You want them nodding along and saying 'that's exactly me' by the end of it."

The Core Explanation
Say this
"Before we look at your intake form, I want to explain how I work — because it's different from most coaching you've probably seen. Most programs focus on one thing: usually food. And that's why they fail. Not because nutrition doesn't matter — it absolutely does — but because your body doesn't live in isolation. How you sleep affects what you crave. Who you spend time with affects what you eat. How stressed you are affects whether any of it works at all."
Continue with
"The Powers Pillar Method is built around four pillars that all talk to each other. Nutrition — how you fuel your body. Lifestyle — how you move and manage your days. Sleep — how you recover. And Relationships — the most underestimated one — how you connect and who supports you. We're going to work on all four, all the time, starting today."
Make it Personal to Them
Ask this
"Just hearing those four pillars — Nutrition, Lifestyle, Sleep, Relationships — which one surprised you the most? Which one feels most uncomfortable to look at?"
Why this question matters
What to listen for
The pillar they resist most is usually the one with the most opportunity. Note it. You'll revisit it in Weeks 8–9 when you focus on Relationships and self-work.
If they say "Relationships"
"Most people say that. It's the one nobody talks about in wellness — but it quietly runs everything. We'll get there together."
Set Expectations
Say this
"Here's what I need from you over the next 12 weeks: honesty over perfection. I don't need you to do everything right. I need you to tell me when things aren't working so we can adjust. This isn't a pass/fail program — it's a process. Some weeks will be hard. That's not failure. That's data. Deal?"

Intake Form Review

⏱ 12–25 minutes

"This is the heart of Session 1. You're not reading the form to them — you're having a conversation based on what they wrote. Look for patterns, not just answers."

How to Approach the Review
Do this
Open their completed intake form. Scan it quickly before diving in. Identify: their lowest-scoring pillar, the most emotionally charged answer, and any answer that surprised you. Start with one of those three — not question 1.
Opening line
"I've read through everything you wrote and I want to say — thank you for your honesty. A few things really stood out to me. Can I share what I noticed?"
Key Questions to Explore (click to mark as asked)
1
"You mentioned [X from their form]. Tell me more about that — what does that look like in a typical week?"
Opens up their reality. Gets specifics rather than generalities.
✓
2
"When you think about your lowest-scoring pillar — when did that start? Was there a time in your life when that felt easier?"
Connects present struggles to history. Often reveals a trigger point.
✓
3
"What does a really good day feel like for you? Describe it in detail — physically, mentally, emotionally."
Establishes the vision. This becomes their north star.
✓
4
"What usually gets in the way? What's your version of falling off the wagon?"
Identifies their specific relapse pattern so you can plan for it proactively.
✓
5
"Is there anything in your form that you want to say more about — anything you held back?"
Creates safety for what wasn't written. Often the most important answer.
✓
Coach Tips for This Section
👂
Listen at 80%
You should be talking 20% of the time in this section. Ask, then be silent. Resist the urge to offer solutions yet.
🔁
Reflect Back
After they speak, say "What I'm hearing is..." and reflect their words back. This builds trust faster than any advice.
📝
Take Notes Openly
"I'm writing this down because it's important" signals that you value what they say. Don't hide note-taking.
⚡
Follow the Energy
When they light up or their voice changes — follow that thread. That's where the real work lives.

Goal Setting

⏱ 25–35 minutes

"Goals set in the first session become the compass for the next 12 weeks. They need to be emotional, specific, and owned by the client — not handed to them by you."

The Goal-Setting Framework
Say this
"I want us to set three goals together — one for each of the next three phases of our work. But I want these to be your goals, not my goals for you. So I'm going to ask you some questions and we'll build them from your answers."
Goal-Setting Questions (click to mark as asked)
1
"In 12 weeks, when this program is done, what do you want to be able to say is different? Not what you want to weigh — how do you want to feel?"
Gets the emotional goal beneath the surface goal.
✓
2
"Which pillar — if you improved it significantly — would change the most about your daily life?"
Identifies their highest-leverage starting point.
✓
3
"What's one thing you want to stop doing, one thing you want to start doing, and one thing you want to feel differently about?"
Creates a stop/start/feel framework that covers behavior and mindset.
✓
Write Their 3 Goals Together
Do this
Based on their answers, co-write 3 goals with them. Each goal should: start with "By Week 12 I will...", be specific and emotionally meaningful, touch at least one pillar. Read them back and ask: "Does that feel true to you? Is that really what you want?"
Anchor the Commitment
Ask this
"On a scale of 1–10, how committed are you to these goals right now?"
How to respond
They say 7 or below
"What would need to be different for it to be a 9?" — then adjust the goals or address the blocker directly.
They say 8–10
"Good. I'm going to remind you of that number on the hard weeks. Screenshot these goals and put them somewhere you'll see them."

How to Frame the Homework
Say this
"This week's homework is about awareness, not action. I don't want you to change anything yet. I want you to observe — like a scientist studying your own life. Everything you notice this week becomes data we use in Session 2. There are no bad results. There's only information."
Coach note
Send the worksheets immediately after the call — while the energy is high. Include a personal note referencing something specific they said today. This touch point matters more than most coaches realize.
Check-In Protocol
Say this
"Between now and our next session, I'll check in mid-week — just a quick message to see how the journaling is going. If anything feels hard or confusing, reach out. You're not doing this alone."

The Powerful Close

⏱ 42–45 minutes

"The last 3 minutes are what they'll remember most. End with intention. Leave them feeling seen, capable, and genuinely excited for what's ahead."

The Closing Script
Say this
"Before we close, I want to say something. I've sat with a lot of people in this seat, and what you shared today took courage. The fact that you showed up, that you were honest, that you said yes to this — that already tells me something about who you are. You're someone who takes their health seriously. Let's honor that over the next 12 weeks."
Final question
"One last thing — what's the one word you want to feel like at the end of our 12 weeks together?"
Coach note
Write down their word
This becomes your secret weapon. Reference it on hard weeks. End Week 12 by asking: "Do you feel [their word] now?" The answer is almost always yes — and it's one of the most powerful moments in coaching.
Send-off
"I'll send your worksheets and our session notes over in the next hour. Our next session is [date/time]. I'm genuinely excited to see what you discover this week. Go be curious about yourself."
